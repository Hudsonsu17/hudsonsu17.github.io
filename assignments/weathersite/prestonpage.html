Preston Falls Short of Four-Peat

Idaho State Journal

Michael Guise

Cooper Hobson sunk into a chair on the bench before placing his hands on top of his forehead and running his fingers through his hair.
Hobson was next to an already emotional Ty Hyde, whose glassy eyes were red from tears. The two stood up with their teammates to grab the red trophy, hardware the squad had become unfamiliar with.
Preston accepted the 4A state runner-up trophy Saturday at the Ford Idaho Center after losing 66-57 to Idaho Falls in overtime of the state championship game. The Indians' run as three-time defending 4A state champions came to an end as the Tigers won their first title since 1988.
